📚 Understanding Air Quality

Learn about air pollutants, their health impacts, and how to protect yourself

🔬 Major Pollutants

PM2.5 (Fine Particulate Matter)

Particles smaller than 2.5 micrometers. Can penetrate deep into lungs and bloodstream.
Main sources: Vehicle emissions, construction dust, industrial activities.
Health impact: Respiratory diseases, cardiovascular problems, reduced lung function.

PM10 (Coarse Particulate Matter)

Particles smaller than 10 micrometers. Irritate airways and cause breathing difficulties.
Main sources: Road dust, crushing operations, pollen.
Health impact: Asthma attacks, bronchitis, reduced lung function.

NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide)

Reddish-brown gas with a characteristic sharp, biting odor.
Main sources: Vehicle exhaust, power plants, industrial facilities.
Health impact: Inflammation of airways, reduced immunity to lung infections.

O₃ (Ozone)

Ground-level ozone formed by chemical reactions between pollutants in sunlight.
Main sources: Vehicle emissions + sunlight reaction.
Health impact: Chest pain, coughing, throat irritation, airway inflammation.

🎨 AQI Categories & Health Advice

PM2.5 Range (µg/m³) Category Health Advice
0-50 Good Air quality is satisfactory. Enjoy outdoor activities!
51-100 Moderate Acceptable for most. Sensitive individuals should limit prolonged outdoor exertion.
101-150 Unhealthy for Sensitive Children, elderly, and people with respiratory conditions should reduce outdoor activities.
151-200 Unhealthy Everyone should avoid prolonged outdoor activities. Wear masks outdoors.
200+ Hazardous Health alert! Stay indoors, use air purifiers, wear N95 masks if going outside.

🛡️ Protection Tips

✅ What You Should Do

  • Check AQI daily before outdoor activities
  • Use air purifiers indoors
  • Wear N95 masks when AQI is high
  • Keep windows closed on high pollution days
  • Stay hydrated
  • Exercise indoors when pollution is high
  • Use public transport or carpool
  • Plant air-purifying indoor plants

❌ What You Should Avoid

  • Outdoor exercise during peak pollution hours
  • Traffic-heavy routes
  • Open burning of waste
  • Prolonged exposure without masks
  • Smoking (worsens air quality)
  • Congested areas during rush hours
  • Construction sites without protection
  • Using wood-burning stoves

📱 About This Project

Atmos is a hybrid air quality monitoring system designed to provide comprehensive, real-time pollution data across the city at a fraction of traditional monitoring costs. Using optimized sensor placement algorithms and mid-grade sensors, we achieve better coverage than the existing 14 CAAQMS stations at just 1% of the cost.

Technology Stack: FastAPI backend, optimization algorithms (Bounding Phase + Golden Ratio Search), real-time WAQI API integration, and interactive data visualization.

Key Features

  • ✅ 67 sensors (4 reference + 63 monitoring)
  • ✅ Scientific overlap calculation for realistic coverage
  • ✅ Priority-based sensor allocation
  • ✅ Live traffic data integration
  • ✅ Real-time AQI monitoring
  • ✅ Cost-effective solution (₹24.9L vs ₹20-25Cr)
